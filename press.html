Long bio ~250 words

Andrew Trotter is a seventeen-year-old composer and pianist whose catalog reaches across orchestral, chamber, and solo piano forms. His independently released body of work includes a symphony, a symphonic poem, a ballet score, a sacred orchestral collection, and intimate writing for piano alone — work released across multiple albums and singles, with new material in progress.

His larger works include Symphony No. 1 "Messiah" (Op. 3), the symphonic poem Tempesta dell'Anima (Op. 2), and the ballet The Silver Swan (Op. 6). His solo piano writing — Piano, Vol. 1, Lullaby, and I love you more — sits alongside chamber writing for piano and cello (Presenze Silenziose) and piano with string quartet (Spring Dance).

His work has been recognized in regional competitions, including a first-place finish at the Georgia Music Festival and a second place at the Bob Jones University piano competition. Beyond composing, he performs as a pianist for sacred services, private events, and recital collaborations across metro Atlanta.

His music is written to be sat with rather than skimmed — drawing on the line that runs from Bach and the European masters through the cinematic composers of today, and shaped by a faith and family tradition that runs underneath the work without speaking over it. He continues to study, perform, and release new work independently.
